waterstones, london

all of the books imaginable!

Waterstones, formerly Waterstone's, is a British book retailer that operates 283 shops, mainly in the United Kingdom and also other nearby countries. As of February 2014, it employs around 3,500 staff in the UK and Europe. An average-sized Waterstones shop sells a range of approximately 30,000 individual books, as well as stationery and other related products. It is said to be the greatest bookstore in the world, founded by Tim Waterstone. Sir Tim Waterstone is a British businessman and author, who is currently 82 years old! If you ever want to visit this beautiful shop, make sure to go to England and have some time looking around this never ending loophole of a vast selection of many brilliant books by many different authors!



review on the books

Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows
Excellent book to end the magical journey of Harry Potter.

JK Rowling finished the groundbreaking series with a remarkable resolution. Harry Potter and The Deathly Hallows concludes all the loose ends and plot points, leaving just enough mystery to allow the reader to feel satisfied yet still desiring for more in the perfect way with her second to best novel in the series. To completely understand this, you have to read the books prior to this one, so it all makes sense. Harry Potter and The Deathly Hallows is a tremendous mix of slow parts in some places and non stop action in others. The end of the saga wraps up the final questions and character arcs and really gives Ron a chance to shine. The morals of Dumbledore are additionally questioned in a way that doesn't make him feel like a completely separate character, but rather a character who was hiding his completely separate character from others. I also full under the group that had no problem with the epilogue. Though I do think Rowling should have stuck to either honoring Dumbledore or Snape, not both by naming Harry's son "Albus Severus Potter", that just seemed a bit much. There are some plot points that also feel like a bit of a work around was used or points that Rowling asks us to suspend our disbelief and not just in a magical sort of way, but as an author she's earned our trust at this points and the series has at well so those moments didn't necessarily ruin the book for me. I also felt that there were some characters and plot points that Rowling had to clarify after the series ended when I'd rather have had it clarified in The Deathly Hallows instead.

Percy Jackson and the Lightning Thief
The best way to start any book series.

Percy Jackson is a great book by Rick Riordan. I found this book out of the series very interesting and if like me you also love the Greek and Roman mythology as much as I do you will realize how amazing the book is. I found the addition of monsters and greek gods to the life of a boy living in the present world very great. The pace of introducing new characters to the story was perfect. Percy, who was a normal kid found out that he was a demigod, or a half blood and the son of Poseidon, god of the sea and went to camp half-blood to embark on a quest to return Zeus' master bolt, who thought that Percy was the lightning thief, or the person who stole his bolt after his mother had been burnt to crisp by a monster. Annabeth Chase and Underwood help him on t his quest. During his quest, he met many people like Medusa, Ares, etc. and also found three pearls. When he went to the underworld, which was controlled by Hades, he went to confront him, but as he did, the lightning bolt appeared in his bag. Hades threatened the three of them, and told that if he gave the bolt, he would returned his mother, however Percy didn't comply and used the pearls to get out of there. He later went to Olympus, which is said to be on the 600th floor in the Empire State Building, and handed over the bolt to Zeus, while Poseidon congratulated him. Overall, it was an amazing way to start the series, and I loved it!

Adventure of a Life: A journey beyond belief
The best way to start any book series.

This is the first book from Harshith.T In this book, you will dive deep into a world full of mysteries and adventures. Things have took a certain flip in LA, as two boys try to find their way through through a series of disasters. In LA, it was supposed to be Earthquake day, as things changed really things. During this period of time, multiple disasters, literally, try to destroy the city. As these three boys try to thrive, other matters get into their hands, as they experience loss. The disastrous disaster end in destruction and chaos. In the second part of this book, the two boys are living normally, until disaster strikes...AGAIN. They heard about a former rival they encountered, which they once thought to be their friend, planning to destroy the moon using magic and power as they now continue their journey through madness, in order to destroy what remains of their enemy, until they meet a new companion, who is pretty helpful in order to stop the war and the project to destroy the moon. These kids still prevail, through disasters striking. This book consists of- Adventure, Action, Humor, Loss and most importantly- DEATH Join these two boys, as they go through the best adventure of their lives!
